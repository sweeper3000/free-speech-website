Free Speech
  • Saudi Arabia

    Saudi Arabia is one of the worst places for free speech. Media is tightly controlled, to the point where the internet is the only place to share ideas freely, but not without surveillance on those who dare to question the government and the religion of Islam.

    The King of Saudi Arabia is on Reporters Without Border's Predators of Press Freedom list.

Raif Badawi

Founder of the blog Liberal Saudi Network, Raif Badawi was convicted in 2014 on the charge of insulting Islam. He was sentenced to:

  • 10 years in jail
  • 1 000 lashes
  • A large fine of an unknown amount
  • 10 year ban on foreign travel after serving his sentance

Being jailed for insulting a religion, which would be considered more or less subjective, is obviously a threat to free speech, but encourages self censorship and blocks debate about religion.

There are currently 10 citizen journalists in jail in Saudi Arabia on charges that include blasphermy, insulting the king, insulting religion and "inciting chaos".

  • Reporters Without Borders

    Reporters Without Borders is commited to freedom of speech, the press and expression. They watch nations and report on their record in this area.

In this particular case, Reporters Without Borders has called attention to this case, including asking Boris Johnson to call for Raif's release when he visited Saudi Arabia

Some other activities include:

  • Protect journalists such as providing bulletproof helmets and vests
  • Training in digital security
  • Provides legal assistance to journalists
  • Helps willing countries draft legislation that protects basic rights to an international standard
  • Publishes the World Press Freedom Index, ranking countries according to freedom of expression and freedom of the press and has incited reaction from the most free to the least free countries
